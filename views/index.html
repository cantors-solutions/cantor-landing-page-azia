Frequently asked questions

How is our analysis done?
Cantor analysis combines technical market analysis with on-chain analysis of a cryptoasset using a machine learning algorithm. Currently there are 14 standardized parameters that we use to create the Cantor score with many others to come:
  1. Adoption (unique and active addresses over time)
  2. Price
  3. Time in the market
  4. Market capitalization
  5. Correlation with BTC
  6. Exponential Moving Averages (EMA)
  7. Relative Strength Index (RSI)
  8. Logarithmic regression and extensions
  9. Return
  10. Volatility
  11. Sharpe ratio
  12. Sortino ratio
  13. Max drawdown
  14. Value at Risk
All these parameters are inserted into the algorithm and analyzed with the help of ML technology. The first series of tests serves to determine the speed and scope of growth of a crypto project, as well as its dynamics in unique and active addresses. In this way, Cantor defines which of them are stagnant and which show strong fundamental parameters. The next step in the analysis is used to measure the relationship between their fundamental score and the market price. Once the price/performance ratio of a project is determined, a deeper analysis of its price movements in the market is performed. Finally, an easy-to-understand score is created that takes all of this information into account. Cryptoassets are then ranked between each other based on their project quality.

This is a unique market analysis designed specifically for long-term investors. Cantor is an unbiased, revolutionary, and accurate scoring system that performs analysis no human could do individually. Our mission is to prevent large losses from people entering the crypto market and help the market identify quality projects.

Who benefits from Cantor?
The crypto market is still relatively small, and therefore the asset volatility is high. Most new investors often lose money when they first come into contact with this market, as most encounter this industry during the media hype when asset prices have already reached almost maximum values.

The Cantor score recognizes which cryptocurrencies have reached or are moving in the maximum price range and calls for caution. Moreover, those currencies that have a good Cantor score also have sound fundamental parameters, so in addition to the reduced risk of a drastic drop in prices, there is also the potential for satisfactory future growth. In this way, Cantor has combined both important investment factors - risk management and potential profit - into one easy-to-use and completely unbiased score.
